History and Heritage

The Birthplace of Empires

Turkije's rich history is intertwined with the rise and fall of great empires. From the ancient Hittites, who ruled Anatolia around 1600 BCE, to the grandeur of the Roman and Byzantine Empires, the region has long been a crossroads of civilizations. One of the most remarkable legacies is Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul), which became a center of culture, trade, and power during the Byzantine era. This unique heritage is evident in landmarks like Hagia Sophia and the Hippodrome.

The Ottoman Empire: A Global Power

The Ottoman Empire, founded in 1299 by Osman I, grew to become one of history's most influential and long-lasting empires. At its height in the 16th and 17th centuries, it spanned three continents, blending cultures and traditions under one rule. The empire’s architectural achievements, such as the Blue Mosque and Topkapı Palace, stand as testaments to its cultural and political dominance. The Ottomans also left an indelible mark on art, cuisine, and governance that continues to shape modern Turkije.

The Turkish Republic: A New Era

The early 20th century marked a turning point in Türkiye’s history. Following the collapse of the Ottoman Empire after World War I, Mustafa Kemal Atatürk led the country through a period of reform and modernization. In 1923, the Republic of Turkije was established, with Atatürk as its first president. His visionary leadership introduced secularism, education reform, and industrial growth, laying the foundations for the modern, dynamic nation we know today.

Turkije: A Vision for the Future

As Turkije stands at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, its future is as dynamic as its rich history. The country has embraced modernization while preserving its cultural heritage, focusing on economic growth, technological innovation, and sustainability. Turkije's Vision 2053 initiative aims to position the nation as a global hub for trade, energy, and tourism, leveraging its strategic location and expanding infrastructure.
